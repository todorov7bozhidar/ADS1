Fourth Wing — by Rebecca Yarros

Violet Sorrengail was meant to live a quiet life surrounded by books—not fight for survival in a place designed to kill her.

But Basgiath War College doesn’t care what she was meant for.

Thrown into the Riders Quadrant by her commanding mother, Violet enters a world where weakness is hunted, failure is fatal, and survival is never guaranteed. The cadets around her are stronger, faster, and far more prepared—and they won’t hesitate to eliminate competition.

Because at Basgiath, students don’t just drop out.

They die.

To make it through, Violet must do the impossible: bond with a dragon—creatures who burn the unworthy to ash without hesitation. Every step forward is a risk. Every decision could be her last.

And then there’s Xaden Riorson.

A leader forged by rebellion. A man whose past is tangled with her family’s in ways that make him as dangerous as the war itself. He should hate her. He has every reason to.

And he does.

At first.

Their connection is sharp, tense, unpredictable—built on mistrust, power, and something neither of them can control. He watches her like a threat. She challenges him like she has nothing left to lose. And somewhere between survival and war, that tension becomes something deeper.

Because the real danger isn’t just the training.

It’s the secrets buried beneath it.

And trusting the wrong person in a place like this?

That’s how you die.
