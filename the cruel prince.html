The Cruel Prince — by Holly Black

 

Jude Duarte doesn’t belong in Faerie—and everyone makes sure she remembers it.

Taken from the human world after witnessing her parents’ murder, she is raised among creatures who see her as lesser, fragile, disposable. Immortals who lie as easily as they breathe. Who take pleasure in cruelty. Who understand power in a way humans never could.

But Jude learns.

She learns how to fight like them. Think like them. Lie like them.

Because if she can’t belong, she’ll rise anyway.

At the center of her torment is Prince Cardan—beautiful, vicious, and impossible to predict. He doesn’t just dislike Jude. He targets her. Pushes her. Breaks her down piece by piece, turning every interaction into a battlefield. Their dynamic is sharp, volatile—built on humiliation, defiance, and something far more complicated beneath the surface.

Because hatred like theirs doesn’t stay simple.

As court politics grow more dangerous, Jude finds herself pulled into a world of shifting alliances, betrayals, and silent wars fought behind smiles and crowns. Power isn’t given—it’s taken. And Jude is willing to become something ruthless enough to claim it.

Even if it means becoming just as cruel as the world that tried to destroy her.

 And even if the one person she can’t trust… is the one she can’t stop wanting.

 

Vibe: Dark fae politics + true enemies-to-lovers + manipulation, ambition, and tension that feels like a knife at your throat.
