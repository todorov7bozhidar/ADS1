Divine Rivals — by Rebecca Ross

The world is at war—but for Iris Winnow, the battle feels personal.

With her brother missing on the front lines and her home slowly falling apart, Iris clings to the one thing she can control: her words. Writing is her escape, her purpose, her way forward in a world that feels like it’s unraveling.

And standing in her way is Roman Kitt.

Her rival. Her competition. The one person who always seems one step ahead.

In the newsroom, their relationship is sharp and distant—defined by quiet tension and unspoken frustration. But beyond that world, something impossible is happening. Letters begin to appear. Anonymous. Honest. Vulnerable.

And through them, everything changes.

Without knowing who they’re truly writing to, they begin to understand each other in ways they never could face-to-face. Walls come down. Truth replaces rivalry. And slowly, something deeper takes root—something built not on attraction, but on connection.

But reality doesn’t stay separate forever.

When Iris is sent to the front lines, the war becomes more than background noise—it becomes survival. And the fragile bond they’ve built is tested by distance, danger, and truths that can no longer stay hidden.

Because love born in words is one thing.

But surviving it in a broken world?

That’s something else entirely.

Vibe: Soft, aching romance + rivals-to-lovers + emotional intimacy that builds slowly and hits hard.
